## Design Context

### Users
Solo developers running 2-4 AI coding agents (Claude Code, OpenCode, Gemini, Codex) simultaneously in separate terminal tabs. They're multitasking — writing code, reviewing output, switching contexts. The island is a peripheral awareness tool they glance at, not something they stare at.

### Brand Personality
Playful, compact, nostalgic — inspired by pixel art and retro games. The pixel knights are central to the identity: each agent gets a tiny character with a unique weapon. The island should feel like a miniature living world watching over your agents, not a corporate monitoring tool.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Delightful companion — a tiny, alive-feeling overlay that reacts to what your agents are doing. When you glance at it, it should feel like checking on little adventurers.
- **Theme**: Both dark and light modes with a toggle. Dark mode is the primary context (used alongside dark terminals). Light mode should feel equally crafted, not an afterthought.
- **Visual language**: Evolved pixel art — retain the knight metaphor but refine the rendering, animations, and overall polish. The pixel aesthetic should feel intentional and charming, not primitive.
- **Agent colors**: Refine the existing palette (purple=Claude, orange=OpenCode, blue=Gemini, green=Codex) for better perceptual uniformity and contrast. Use OKLCH for consistent lightness across agent colors.
- **Anti-references**: No corporate dashboard aesthetics. No gradients, glassmorphism, thin fonts, or generic AI visuals (chat bubbles, sparkles). Not barebones minimal. Not overly realistic.

### Design Principles
1. **Glanceable above all** — information must be readable in under 1 second at 340px wide. Status, agent, and activity at a glance.
2. **Compact personality** — every pixel earns its place. The island is small; density is a virtue, but never at the cost of charm.
3. **Living and reactive** — the knights should feel alive. Movement, status changes, and transitions should be delightful without being distracting.
4. **Dark-first, light-equal** — design for the dark terminal context first, but the light theme must feel equally considered and polished.
5. **Accessible by default** — WCAG AAA compliance. High contrast ratios, reduced motion support, keyboard navigation, screen reader friendly.
